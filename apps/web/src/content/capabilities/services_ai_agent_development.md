faqs:
  - category: About AI Agents
    questions:
      - question: What is an AI agent and how is it different from a chatbot or copilot?
        answer: A chatbot answers questions in a conversation window. A copilot assists a human who is doing the work. An AI agent acts autonomously—it receives a goal, reasons through the steps required, uses tools and data to complete those steps, and produces a result without a human directing each action. Agents can browse the web, call APIs, read and write documents, query databases, send messages, and take actions inside connected systems. The distinction matters because agents handle multi-step, decision-intensive work that chatbots and copilots cannot—document review with conditional actions, lead qualification across a CRM and email thread, or report generation from multiple data sources assembled and synthesised without human coordination.
      - question: What kinds of tasks are AI agents best suited for?
        answer: AI agents perform best on tasks that are high-volume, multi-step, require access to data from multiple sources, and follow a definable logic even when that logic branches. Examples include document processing (extract, classify, route, summarise), research and synthesis (gather information from multiple sources, identify patterns, produce structured outputs), customer service triage (qualify inbound requests, retrieve relevant information, escalate with context), lead qualification (enrich leads from multiple data points, score against criteria, update CRM, trigger follow-up), and operational reporting (aggregate data from connected tools, generate structured reports on schedule). Tasks requiring nuanced human judgment, sensitive relationship management, or creative decisions with no right answer are not suited for agents—those need humans, supported by AI where useful.
      - question: Do AI agents replace staff?
        answer: Not by design, and we build with explicit intent to augment rather than eliminate. The most durable implementations delegate high-volume, repetitive, low-judgment tasks to agents—freeing your team for the work that requires context, relationship, and judgment that AI cannot replicate. In practice, organisations that deploy agents well don't reduce headcount in the first implementation cycle. They increase what the same team can handle—more clients, faster response, higher quality output—before headcount conversations become relevant. We surface this distinction explicitly in every engagement because how you frame AI adoption internally determines whether your team supports or undermines the implementation.

        answer: Because the most common cause of failed AI agent implementations is building the wrong thing. Organisations frequently identify an attractive use case without assessing whether their data is clean enough to power it, whether the relevant systems have accessible APIs, or whether the workflow logic is actually consistent enough to automate. Discovery surfaces these constraints before development begins—saving significantly more than it costs. A $5,000 discovery engagement that redirects you from a technically infeasible build to a high-impact one that works pays for itself in the first month of operation.       - question: How does Madavi approach data privacy and compliance in AI agent builds?
        answer: Data privacy and compliance are assessed during discovery and designed into architecture, not treated as a checklist at deployment. We review which data the agent will access, process, and store against applicable frameworks—the Kenya Data Protection Act, GDPR where relevant for international-facing organisations, and sector-specific regulations for healthcare and legal clients. Practical measures include data minimisation (the agent accesses only what it needs), audit logging (every agent action is traceable), human-in-the-loop checkpoints for decisions with significant consequences, and clear data retention policies. We also assess bias in agent outputs where decisions affect people—hiring, customer service routing, credit-related processes—and document mitigation approaches. Compliance documentation is included in the handover package.
