    questions:
      - question: What does an SEO service actually include month to month?
        answer: Monthly SEO management covers four workstreams. Technical maintenance—monitoring and resolving site health issues, Core Web Vitals, indexation, and crawl performance. On-page optimization—systematically improving metadata, heading structure, internal linking, and content relevance across priority pages. Content SEO—keyword-targeted article and landing page production following an agreed editorial calendar. Reporting and strategy—monthly performance review covering rankings, organic traffic, conversions, and next-month priorities. Exact scope varies by engagement tier; every retainer starts with an audit establishing the baseline and priority order.
      - question: How long does SEO take to show results?
        answer: Meaningful organic traffic gains typically emerge within 3–6 months of consistent execution. Technical fixes and on-page optimization on existing pages can produce ranking improvements in 4–8 weeks. New content takes longer—most articles reach stable ranking positions 3–6 months after publication as search engines crawl, index, and assess them against competing content. Local SEO visibility improvements (map pack, Google Business) often appear within 6–10 weeks. SEO compounds over time—month 12 typically outperforms month 6 significantly, and the asset value of a well-optimized site continues growing after active investment slows.
      - question: Is SEO relevant for businesses targeting African markets?
        answer: Absolutely—and the opportunity is often greater here than in Western markets. Search behaviour in Kenya and across Africa continues to grow rapidly, keyword competition in many verticals remains lower than comparable markets in the UK or US, and many local competitors have not invested seriously in SEO. Businesses that build organic visibility now benefit from compounding advantage as the market matures. Local SEO in particular is underinvested by most African businesses—map pack rankings and local intent searches represent significant untapped visibility for professional services, healthcare, legal, and other industries we work in.
  - category: Technical SEO
    questions:
      - question: How do we know if our site has technical SEO problems?
        answer: Technical SEO issues are usually invisible to the naked eye but visible in a crawl audit. Common indicators include declining organic traffic without obvious content changes, pages not appearing in search results despite good content, slow page load times (especially on mobile), crawl errors showing up in Google Search Console, and a sitemap that doesn't accurately reflect current site structure. Our SEO Foundation Audit surfaces all of these—indexed pages, crawl errors, Core Web Vitals scores, duplicate content, canonical problems, structured data issues, and internal linking gaps—with an impact-ordered fix list.
      - question: Do you fix technical issues or just identify them?
        answer: Both. Audit engagements deliver a complete diagnostic with implementation guidance your development team can act on, or that we can execute if you prefer. Ongoing SEO management includes technical maintenance as a standing workstream—we monitor, identify, and resolve issues as they arise rather than waiting for a periodic audit. For technical fixes requiring development access (site architecture changes, page speed improvements, structured data implementation), we work directly with your dev team or, if needed, our web development partners.
  - category: Content SEO
    questions:
      - question: What makes content SEO different from just publishing blog posts?
        answer: Content SEO starts with keyword and intent research—understanding what your audience is searching for, what stage of the buying journey each query represents, and what content already exists for each term. Every piece of content is assigned to a target keyword cluster, written to address a specific audience question, structured for both readability and search signals, and optimized before publication. Content is then linked into the site architecture intentionally, not just published and abandoned. The result is content that ranks and converts rather than content that exists.
      - question: How much content do you produce per month?
        answer: Production volume depends on engagement tier and goals. A Content SEO Sprint engagement typically produces 6–10 long-form articles over 8–12 weeks. Monthly SEO retainers include 2–4 pieces of keyword-targeted content at baseline, scaling up for clients treating content as a primary growth channel. Quality and strategic fit take precedence over volume—one well-researched, properly optimized 1,500-word article on a high-intent keyword outperforms five generic posts on topics with no real search demand.
  - category: Local SEO
    questions:
      - question: What is local SEO and why does it matter in Kenya?
        answer: Local SEO is optimization for searches with geographic intent—"law firm in Nairobi," "healthcare clinic Westlands," "accountant near me." It covers Google Business Profile optimization, local keyword targeting, location-specific landing pages, citation consistency across directories, and map pack ranking. In Kenya, local SEO matters for any business serving customers by geography—professional services, healthcare, retail, hospitality. Many businesses in these sectors are underinvested in local SEO, which means the opportunity to dominate map pack results and local intent searches is real and achievable within 2–3 months of focused effort.
      - question: Do you manage multi-location local SEO?
        answer: Yes. Multi-location SEO requires consistent Google Business Profiles for each location, location-specific landing pages optimized for city or area-level keywords, and unified citation management ensuring name, address, and phone number (NAP) data is consistent across all directories. We build and maintain this infrastructure for professional firms and businesses with presence across multiple Nairobi neighbourhoods or multiple African cities—ensuring each location competes effectively in its local market rather than cannibalizing a single consolidated ranking.
  - category: AI Search & GEO
    questions:
      - question: Should we be worried about AI search eating our organic traffic?
        answer: AI search is reshaping how some search intent is served—AI Overviews, Perplexity, and ChatGPT increasingly answer informational queries without requiring a click. This affects informational content more than transactional or navigational intent. For most businesses, the majority of high-value organic traffic (service pages, product pages, local searches) remains in traditional search and is not significantly impacted today. The appropriate response is not to abandon SEO fundamentals, but to build content that positions you as a citable source in AI-generated answers—clear, authoritative, well-structured content with strong brand signals. We incorporate this into content strategy without treating it as the lead priority until the data in your specific vertical justifies it.
